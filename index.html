EDUCATION NEEDS TO CHANGE

Childern are not taught real-world hard science skills at schools

South Africa, being the second largest economy in Africa, faces a challenge of skills shortage in science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM) fields. It is reported that 80% of all jobs by 2020 will require skills in STEM, but most South Africans are inclined towards business, accounting and social sciences qualifications. There is a lack of interest in STEM among primary and high school students.

So how does Nkanyezi Technologies help make education better?

We firmly believe that providing students with a hands on experience, we can make them passionate about maths, technology, science and engineering, motivating them to work hard and pursue careers in related fields.

WHAT IS GALILEO'S FINGER?

Low cost astronomy learning tool

Galileo’s Finger is a low cost astronomy learning device that teaches scientific principles, hands on engineering skills, and provides a brief introduction to programming and systems thinking, core skills in the hard sciences. Once the device has been set up, students select an extraterrestrial body on the computer and the device points it out in the night sky using a laser pointer. On screen, students can view information about it, in their own language. If what was selected was a galaxy, or perhaps a sign of the zodiac, children will learn about these from their own cultural perspective.
